# HR Questions & Sample Answers

> 💡 These are **frameworks** — replace the examples with your own real experiences.

---

## 1. Tell me about yourself
**Framework:** Present → Past → Future (2 minutes).
> "I'm a Java backend developer with a strong foundation in Spring Boot, REST APIs, and
> databases. Recently I built [project] where I [key achievement]. Before that, I [background/
> education]. I enjoy solving backend problems and writing clean, scalable code. I'm now looking
> for a role where I can grow as a backend engineer and contribute to real products like yours."

✅ Keep it professional, focus on skills + projects, not personal life.

---

## 2. What are your strengths?
Pick 2–3 **relevant** strengths + a quick example.
> "My key strengths are problem-solving and consistency. For example, in [project], I debugged a
> tricky performance issue by identifying an N+1 query and fixing it, cutting response time by
> 60%. I'm also a fast learner — I picked up Docker and deployed my project within a week."

---

## 3. What are your weaknesses?
Be honest + show **improvement**. Never say "I have none" or a fake strength.
> "I used to spend too long perfecting small details, which slowed me down. I've improved by
> setting time limits per task and focusing on delivering working solutions first, then
> refining. It's made me much more productive."

---

## 4. Why should we hire you?
Match your skills to **their needs**.
> "You need a backend developer strong in Java and Spring Boot, which is exactly my core skill
> set. I've built real projects like [project], I learn quickly, and I work well in teams. I'm
> confident I can contribute from day one while continuing to grow."

---

## 5. Why do you want to join this company?
Show you **researched** them.
> "I admire [company]'s work in [product/domain] and your focus on [value/tech]. The role aligns
> with my backend skills, and I'm excited by the scale of problems you solve. I want to grow in a
> place that values engineering quality — which your team clearly does."

✅ Mention something **specific** (product, tech, values).

---

## 6. Explain your project
Use: **Problem → Tech → Your role → Challenge → Result**.
> "I built an e-commerce backend using Spring Boot, JPA, and MySQL. It handles products, cart,
> and orders with JWT authentication. I designed the REST APIs and the order flow. The biggest
> challenge was preventing stock race conditions, which I solved using database transactions and
> optimistic locking. The result was a reliable checkout with no overselling."

---

## 7. Conflict / difficult situation (behavioral)
Use **STAR**: Situation, Task, Action, Result.
> "**Situation:** A teammate and I disagreed on the API design. **Task:** We needed to agree
> quickly to meet a deadline. **Action:** I suggested we list pros and cons of both approaches
> and consulted our senior. **Result:** We picked the cleaner design, delivered on time, and I
> learned the value of data-driven discussion over ego."

---

## 8. Salary expectations
Stay flexible + research the market.
> "I'm open and flexible. Based on my skills and the market rate for this role, I'd expect
> something in the range of [X–Y]. But I'm more focused on the right opportunity and growth, and
> I'm sure we can agree on a fair number."

✅ Give a **range**, not a single number. Never undersell blindly.

---

## 9. Career goals
Show ambition that fits the company.
> "In the short term, I want to become a strong backend engineer, mastering Spring Boot,
> microservices, and system design. In the long term, I'd like to take on architecture and
> mentoring responsibilities. I see this role as a great step toward that."

---

## 10. Where do you see yourself in 5 years?
> "In 5 years, I see myself as a senior backend engineer who owns critical systems, mentors
> juniors, and contributes to architectural decisions — ideally growing here at [company]."

---

## 📋 Quick HR Cheat Sheet
| Question | Key idea |
|----------|----------|
| Tell me about yourself | Present → Past → Future |
| Strengths | 2-3 relevant + example |
| Weakness | Honest + improving |
| Why hire you | Match their needs |
| Why this company | Show research |
| Project | Problem → Tech → Role → Challenge → Result |
| Conflict | STAR method |
| Salary | Give a range, stay flexible |
| Goals | Ambition that fits the role |

---

## ✅ Best Practices
- Smile, stay calm, and be confident.
- Back claims with **real examples**.
- Ask 1–2 thoughtful questions at the end.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes
- Badmouthing previous employers.
- Memorized robotic answers.
- Saying "I don't have any weaknesses."
- Not researching the company.

## 🙋 Questions to Ask Them
- "What does success look like in this role in the first 6 months?"
- "What technologies does the team use?"
- "How is the team structured?"
